#:04
Year: 2021
Project: A Collection of Things
Type: Drawings
Size: N/A
Info: Undergraduate Architectural Thesis 2020-2021. Advisor: Doug Jackson. A Collection of Things* challenges the homogeneous typological processes of living and instead implements non-normative and provocative architectural experiences that can continuously challenge typological profiles of architecture. Moving mechanical characters posited in the non-zoned region of the LA River near the suburbs of South Gate promote user agency through architectural adaptability with the desire of never really creating a new building “type”. This thesis challenges the process of classification and instead provides a more inclusive system of design that is meant to prioritize difference, uniqueness, and social precarity.

#:13
Year: 2023
Project: A Nominal Chair
Type: Drawings
Size: 18"x18"x36"
Info: Buildner International Design Competition; "The Architects Chair". Competition entry with co-designer Inno Yoro. Nominal dimensions refers to the sizes used to describe approximate characteristics of the object rather than the exact dimensions. It may also refer to the thought-process of being more dimensionally conscious of the build and as a result the material waste. More nominal = less wasteful.

Zack is an architectural designer currently living along the Central Coast of California. He recieved his Bachelors of Architecture and Environmental Design from California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo.

Drawings, objects, and buildings are the types of things Zack usually finds himself creating day-to-day. Through graphics, materials, and assemblies, he's interested in exploring ways we can challenge how we design + build in our community.

If you're interested in collaborating on a project please feel free to reach out at the contact below. Zack is a full-service designer and is always interested in new + exciting projects of all types (especially new ones!).

When not in the studio you can probably find Zack outside surfing, trail running, or camping with his dog along the Central Coast.
